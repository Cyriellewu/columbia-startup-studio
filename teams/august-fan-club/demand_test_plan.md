# Evidence of Life — Demand Test Plan

**Team:** August Fan Club  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Landing page:** https://evidenceoflife.vercel.app/  
**Test window:** ~5 days, ~$200 total budget

---

## Audience

**Primary audience:** College students and early-career knowledge workers who struggle with ADHD-style planning pain: time blindness, context switching, unfinished tasks, scattered planning tools, and the feeling that their day disappeared.

**Initial targeting:**
- Location: New York City / Columbia University area as the first reachable test market
- Age: 18–30
- Interests: productivity, ADHD planning, Notion, Google Calendar, journaling, student life, study tools, time blocking, Pomodoro, habit tracking, digital planning
- Behaviors / proxies: students, graduate students, early founders, knowledge workers, heavy planner users, people engaging with productivity or ADHD-related content

**Why this audience:** Evidence of Life is strongest for people who already try to plan their days but still feel disconnected from what actually happened. They often use multiple tools — calendars, notes, reminders, timers, screenshots, paper planners — but none of those tools connects plan, action, evidence, and recap in one place.

---

## Two Angles (A/B)

We are testing two messaging frames against the same audience. Both point to the same landing page. The only variable is the ad creative and copy.

### Angle A — “Your day did not disappear”

**Frame:** ADHD-style time blindness / emotional relief

- **Headline:** Your day did not disappear.
- **Subhead:** Evidence of Life turns scattered tasks, focus sessions, photos, and locations into visible proof of what actually happened.
- **CTA:** Join the Waitlist
- **Visual:** A calm timeline screenshot showing planned time, actual time, and a recap/evidence state.
- **Hypothesis:** Users who struggle with time blindness and scattered planning will respond to emotional language about making the day visible.

### Angle B — “Stop ending the day with a guilty todo list”

**Frame:** Done List instead of To-do List

- **Headline:** Stop ending the day with a guilty todo list.
- **Subhead:** Evidence of Life turns your plans into a living timeline, so unfinished work, focus sessions, and small wins still count.
- **CTA:** Get Early Access
- **Visual:** Before/after creative: a messy task list becoming a clean daily timeline with evidence blocks.
- **Hypothesis:** Users who feel guilty about unfinished tasks will respond to the idea that progress can still be recorded even when tasks are not fully completed.

## Budget

| Item | Budget |
|------|--------|

If we run only one platform first, we will still split creative spend evenly between the two angles.

---

## Success Threshold

**Committed before launch, so results are not rationalized afterward:**

- **Landing-page conversion (visit → email):** ≥ 5% on at least one angle
- **Total signups across both angles:** ≥ 40 waitlist signups
- **Cost per signup:** ≤ $5
- **Angle signal:** the winning angle must outperform the other by at least 30% conversion rate to count as a meaningful signal at this budget

If neither angle clears the threshold, we will revise either the audience or the core message before spending more.
